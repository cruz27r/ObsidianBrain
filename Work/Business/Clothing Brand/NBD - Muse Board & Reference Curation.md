## The Virgil Method — How to Use This Document

> *"Design is like DJing. You take existing sounds and recontextualise them."*
> — Virgil Abloh

The **3% rule**: Take a reference. Change it 3%. Enough to make it yours, not enough to lose the familiarity that makes it resonate. The goal is NOT to copy — it's to find the specific element that carries meaning and transform it through your brand's lens.

The sampling process stays **visible**. Abloh made the references traceable — quotation marks around "AIR," exposed seams, IKEA rugs in a gallery. For NBD: the 326 is already the Virgil move. A number that reads as athletic/collegiate but means something else entirely. Apply that logic everywhere.

**How to work through this document:**
1. Look at the source
2. Identify what specifically works (the one element, not the whole thing)
3. Ask: what does NBD's version of that look like?
4. The answer is almost never "put NBD's logo on it" — it's deeper than that

## Muse Category 1: Black Brand Legacy — The Originators

These brands built the exact audience NBD is targeting, a generation earlier. The audience remembers them, or grew up around people who did. Study them to understand the DNA.

### FUBU — *"For Us, By Us"*
**What it is:** Founded 1989, Queens NY by Daymond John. First Black-owned streetwear brand to go international. Built entirely for the hip hop community — the name itself is the mission statement.
**What to look at:** The logo placement (oversized, chest-dominant), the boldness of ownership ("this is ours"), the community-first business model
**The 3% for NBD:** FUBU said *For Us By Us* — a collective statement. NBD's version is personal, not collective: *Not By Design* is *For Those Still Going, By Someone Who Almost Didn't*. Same energy of ownership, different direction — inward instead of communal.
**Where to find:** [Grailed – Vintage FUBU](https://www.grailed.com/designers/fubu) | [Etsy – Vintage FUBU](https://www.etsy.com/market/karl_kani_fubu)

### Karl Kani — *The Silhouette Architect*
**What it is:** Early 90s LA. Oversized, baggy cuts before oversized was a trend — it was just how the culture dressed. Chest-heavy graphic placement. The proportions are exactly what the NBD silhouette references.
**What to look at:** The chest/back graphic scale, the relaxed drop shoulder before drop shoulder was designed, the collegiate number usage
**The 3% for NBD:** Karl Kani's number graphics were jerseys, sports identity. 326 transforms the number into a coded brand name. Same visual tradition — athletic numerals — completely different meaning underneath.
**Where to find:** [Karl Kani archive – Red Bull article](https://www.redbull.com/int-en/karl-kani-hip-hop-streetwear-fashion-brand-story) | [Grailed – Vintage Karl Kani](https://www.grailed.com/designers/karl-kani)

### Cross Colours — *Message as Design System*
**What it is:** 1989 LA. "Clothing Without Prejudice." Pan-African colors, political messaging embedded in the brand name and garments. The brand statement was the product.
**What to look at:** How the brand name carried the whole philosophy without needing explanation. The message WAS the aesthetic.
**The 3% for NBD:** Cross Colours embedded a political/cultural message into the brand identity itself. NBD does the same with a philosophical one — the 6-step cycle isn't decoration, it's the operating system. What they did with color and collective identity, NBD does with the cycle and perseverance.
**Where to find:** [Cross Colours history – KCRW](https://www.kcrw.com/culture/shows/design-and-architecture/cross-colours-fast-fashions-hidden-costs/early-90s-la-streetwear-label-cross-colours-is-back)

## Muse Category 2: Athletic Archive — The Number Tradition

The 326 logo already draws from this tradition. Go deeper. Understand what made athletic numbers culturally resonant — not just visually, but emotionally.

### Vintage Champion NBA Jerseys (90s)
**What it is:** Champion made NBA jerseys in the 90s. The numbers were identity — Shaq's 32, Iverson's 3, Jordan's 23. A number became a person, a story, a shorthand for something bigger than the player.
**What to look at:** How a number alone — no name needed, no wordmark — carries weight. The mesh fabric, the drop shoulder, the oversized cut on a tee under the jersey. The number AS the statement.
**The 3% for NBD:** 326 is already doing this. Now study *how* those numbers were placed, weighted, colored. The hierarchy: number dominant, name secondary (or absent). Apply that to NBD graphic hierarchy.
**Where to find:** [Pinterest – 90s Champion NBA Jerseys](https://www.pinterest.com/ssthrowbacks/90s-champion-nba-jerseys/) | [Pinterest – 90s NBA Jerseys ideas](https://www.pinterest.com/ideas/90s-nba-jerseys/957032189607/)

### STARTER Jackets & Jerseys
**What it is:** The jacket that meant you were in something. Teams, cities, identity. Wearing a STARTER piece meant allegiance — it was earned, not just purchased.
**What to look at:** The chest placement, the color blocking, the feeling of belonging to something with specific membership. Also: the actual silhouette — oversized, drop shoulder, heavy.
**The 3% for NBD:** STARTER's power was allegiance to a team. NBD's allegiance is to a mindset, not a city or sport. The garment says *I'm in this* — same energy, different cause.
**Where to find:** [Grailed – Vintage STARTER jerseys](https://www.grailed.com/designers/starter/jerseys) | [Pinterest – Retro Jerseys](https://www.pinterest.com/peethebeadmaker/retro-jerseys/)

## Muse Category 3: Current Brands — What's Working Now

### Awake NY — *Graphic Clarity + Message Consistency*
**What it is:** Founded by Angelo Baque (former Supreme brand director). Built since 2012 on bold screen-printed graphics referencing NY cultural history — Michelle Obama, Nelson Mandela, Big L tribute pieces. The message hasn't changed in 14 years. That consistency IS the brand.
**What to look at:** How a single clean graphic on a solid garment does everything. No busy layouts. One statement, executed precisely. Also: their recent Harlem/Big L tribute — how they reference real cultural figures with earned authority.
**The 3% for NBD:** Awake NY's authority comes from rootedness. NBD's rootedness is personal, not geographic. The graphic clarity is the direct muse — one image, one statement, maximum weight.
**Where to find:** [awakenyclothing.com](https://awakenyclothing.com) | [S&S Activewear article on Awake NY](https://thehub.ssactivewear.com/press/why-you-need-to-pay-attention-to-streetwear-brand-awake-ny/)

### Corteiz — *The Alcatraz Model*
**What it is:** London. Alcatraz island logo = rebellion, breaking free. Guerrilla drops, cash-only pop-ups, drops that are events. The brand identity is about belonging to something most people can't access — but not through price, through *being real enough*.
**What to look at:** The logo's embedded meaning — a prison island as a brand symbol for people who feel locked out of the mainstream. The drop model: the purchase is the last step, the experience of getting it IS the product.
**The 3% for NBD:** Corteiz used a physical place (Alcatraz) as a symbol of being locked out. NBD's symbol is a state of being — the rebuild. The outsider identity is the same. The method of earning belonging can mirror Corteiz without copying their model directly.
**Where to find:** [OPUMO – Corteiz story](https://www.opumo.com/magazine/corteiz-meet-the-label-behind-streetwears-second-coming/)

### thisisneverthat x Daniel Johnston — *Outsider Art as Garment*
**What it is:** Korean brand thisisneverthat collaborated with the estate of Daniel Johnston — a cult outsider artist known for raw, emotionally charged drawings. The art was transferred directly to clothing.
**What to look at:** How raw, imperfect artwork carries MORE weight on a garment than polished design. The imperfection is the authenticity. The outsider as the subject, not the observer.
**The 3% for NBD:** The "outsider art as garment" framework applies to NBD. The 6-step cycle isn't polished motivational content — it's raw process. Graphic direction should lean into that rawness, not clean it up.
**Where to find:** [Hypebeast – thisisneverthat x Daniel Johnston](https://hypebeast.com/2025/6/thisisneverthat-daniel-johnston-collaboration-capsule-collection-release-info)

## Muse Category 4: Virgil's Specific Moves — Applied to NBD

These are direct Abloh methods. Each one is a template.

| Virgil's move | What he did | NBD's version |
|---|---|---|
| Quotation marks | Put "AIR" in quotes on the Nike sole — making the familiar strange | Put "THEY'LL NEVER GET IT." in quotes as a graphic. The punctuation does the work. |
| Exposed seams | Cut open the Air Jordan, showed the construction | The 6 steps visible inside a collar, on a hem tape, inside a pocket — the process is part of the product |
| Meme as art | Turned the IKEA FRAKTA bag into an $26 Balenciaga bag | Take the most generic motivational poster language and strip it to nothing. The word FAIL in 72pt type on a chest. Just that. |
| Number as identity | Used Nike's existing naming systems and subverted them | 326 — already done. Now: what's the next number in the system? Does each drop get its own number? |
| Making the reference traceable | Left clues so audiences could find where the inspiration came from | Reference the 90s Black brands explicitly — in copy, in interviews, in naming. "This hoodie is what FUBU started. This is what it became." |

## NBD Graphic Direction — What This All Points To

From all of the above, the graphic language for NBD should:

1. **Use numbers as statements** — 326 is the anchor. Future graphics can extend the number system (each drop, each step in the cycle gets a number treatment)
2. **Be legible from a distance** — Awake NY and the athletic tradition both teach this. One thing, big, clear
3. **Carry rawness** — thisisneverthat x Johnston shows that imperfection is authenticity. NBD graphics don't need to be polished. The 6-step cycle in a slightly uneven hand-set type hits harder than a clean sans-serif
4. **Leave a thread to pull** — Virgil made references traceable. NBD's 326 does this. The viewer who figures out why it's 326 is now a deeper believer than someone who just thinks it looks good
5. **Let the garment breathe** — the best pieces in every reference brand have restraint. One graphic element, executed with full intention, beats four competing ideas
