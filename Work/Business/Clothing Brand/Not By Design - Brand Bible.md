## 0. Origin Story — Why This Brand Exists

> *This section is the brand's foundation. Every design decision traces back here.*

The brand started as **Unknown Views** — built in high school, before the story had weight behind it.

Then life happened. Real challenges. Real setbacks. Real failures. The kind that don't just slow you down — they make you question who you are. There was a full relearning process: of identity, of what mattered, of what it actually takes to keep going when it would be easier to stop.

Not By Design came out of that. Not the fantasy version — the real version. The messy, non-linear, nobody-planned-this version.

**The truth the brand is built on:**

No one's story is perfect. No path is perfectly clean. No success is easily gained. Failure doesn't mean success is impossible — it only makes the story of getting there that much more compelling.

So be delusional. Write the story. Don't end it before it's really done.

---

**Why "Not By Design" specifically:**
It's honest. The name admits that nothing about this came out according to a plan. The success, the path, the brand itself — none of it was by design. And that's not a flaw. That's exactly the point. The imperfection of the journey is the proof of the perseverance.

**The line from Unknown Views to Not By Design:**
One was the beginning — unnamed, unformed, pre-experience. The other is the rebuild after the fall. They aren't separate brands; they're the same person at different points in the cycle.

*Face → Unlearn → Fail → Adapt → Rebuild → Repeat.*
Rafa went through the cycle. Not By Design is what came out the other side.

## 1. Identity

**Brand name:** Not By Design
**Logo mark:** `326` — custom-edited font
> The number is not arbitrary: N-O-T (3) + B-Y (2) + D-E-S-I-G-N (6) = 326. The logo carries the name in code. Only those who look will understand.

**Catchline:** *They'll never get it. Do it anyway.*

**Mission:** Committed to a delusional belief to succeed.

**The 6-Step Cycle** (brand philosophy made wearable):
```
FACE → UNLEARN → FAIL → ADAPT → REBUILD → REPEAT
```
This cycle is both the brand's operating system and a design system. It repeats — because success isn't linear.

**What the brand is NOT:**
- Not punk (attitude is different — quieter, more internal)
- Not motivational-poster generic
- Not a hype brand built on scarcity alone
- Not an ironic brand — it means what it says

---

## 2. Core Feeling

> Fashion as an outlet to push a mission — perseverance, delusional belief in yourself, and in continuing to push forward beyond any failure or setback.

The brand says: success comes as long as you don't accept defeat. You don't have to be winning. You just have to still be moving.

The feeling when someone puts it on: *I'm not lost. I'm still in it.*

---

## 3. Target Customer

**Age:** 18–30
**Where they are in life:** Going into the world, possibly feeling lost, dealing with setbacks, trying to hang in or level up
**Interests:** Hip hop, sports, vintage Y2K/90s culture, streetwear
**Style personality:** Baggy/oversized as a baseline, but can also switch into business casual — they want to show their professional side without suits. The clothes should let them code-switch without losing who they are.
**Mindset:** Outsider. Not accepted by the "right" rooms yet. Knows it. Still going anyway.
**Discovery:** Instagram, word of mouth, music/culture adjacent

---

## 4. Aesthetic Pillars

**Three words:** Perseverance. Grounded. Deliberate.

**Primary aesthetic:** Hybrid — Vintage Y2K/90s inspo + modern minimalism. Not one or the other. The tension between them is the aesthetic.

**Silhouette:** Oversized/baggy primary, but garments that can shift into business casual. Not fashion-casual — *functional versatility* tied to the brand's identity of being ready for whatever comes next.

**Reference brands to study** (what to take from each):
| Brand | What to take | What to leave |
|---|---|---|
| Fear of God / Essentials | Business casual rebellion, premium proportion, versatility | Price point inaccessibility |
| PLEASURES | 90s/Y2K cultural reference embedded in graphics | Their specific rock-music alignment |
| Corteiz (CRTZ) | Outsider identity as strength, community first | Their UK-specific cultural coding |
| Awake NY | Message-driven design, graphic clarity, NY understated | Their limited distribution strategy |
| Denim Tears | Fashion as philosophy/cultural statement | Their history-specific focus |
| Brain Dead | Collage/art-heavy graphics, anti-generic | Their horror/cult aesthetic |

*Not trying to be any of these — studying what they got right.*
